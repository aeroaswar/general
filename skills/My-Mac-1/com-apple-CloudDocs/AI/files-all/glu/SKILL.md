\# Glu (shop-glu.com)  
  
\#\# Entity snapshot  
  
\- \*\*Business\*\*: D2C premium minimalist innerwear / intimate apparel  
\- \*\*Market\*\*: Indonesia (primary), Singapore (planned expansion)  
\- \*\*Platform\*\*: Shopify  
\- \*\*Brand architecture (planned)\*\*: Glu Sleep, Glu Men, Glu Move, Glu Maternity  
\- \*\*Strategic state\*\*: Full brand audit + homepage redesign + two-volume 2026-2030 playbook complete (covering growth, brand architecture, content, retail, community, Singapore runbook)  
  
\#\# Brand identity  
  
\- \*\*Aesthetic\*\*: Premium minimalism. Quiet luxury. Negative space. Earned restraint.  
\- \*\*Voice\*\*: Confident without proving it. Calm. Direct. Never cute, never loud.  
\- \*\*Visual\*\*: Restrained palette. Editorial photography. No emoji-heavy social. No trend-chasing.  
\- \*\*What Glu is NOT\*\*: Fast fashion, micro-influencer-driven, cheap, "fun", price-led, novelty-driven  
  
\#\# Operating context  
  
\- \*\*Margin reality\*\*: D2C in Indonesia is margin-sensitive — landed cost, shipping, returns, COGS all matter  
\- \*\*Channel mix\*\*: Shopify D2C primary; social commerce (IG, TikTok Shop) ancillary; potential offline pop-up + retail down the line  
\- \*\*Customer segments\*\*: 25-40 y/o urban, design-conscious, brand-aware, willing to pay for quality and aesthetic  
\- \*\*Singapore expansion runbook\*\*: detailed legal, logistics, press, creator seeding, pop-up venue plan already exists — reference don't recreate  
\- \*\*Content cadence\*\*: prioritise depth over volume; one strong piece \> five mediocre  
  
\#\# Default working assumptions  
  
\- Pricing is premium-anchored, not competitor-anchored — discount language is brand-toxic  
\- Photography is the single highest-leverage asset — quality threshold matters more than quantity  
\- AOV improvement comes from bundle architecture, not BOGO mechanics  
\- Community precedes paid acquisition for premium D2C  
\- Retail (offline) is a brand-amplification play, not primarily a revenue play  
  
\#\# Tone & output  
  
\- For brand work: short, declarative, restrained. Rubin-discipline.  
\- For ops/numbers: tables and unit economics math, no hedging  
\- For content: focus on one truth per piece, no listicles  
\- Indonesian for customer-facing, English/bilingual for internal strategy  
  
\#\# What to NOT do  
  
\- Never recommend discounting as a primary lever  
\- Don't suggest brand additions (new sub-line, new category) without subtracting something else  
\- Don't borrow aesthetic moves from fast fashion or trend retailers — protect the quiet  
\- Don't pad copy with adjectives; Glu's voice is noun-led  
\- Don't suggest features when subtraction would serve  
  
\#\# Common request types  
  
\- Pricing / bundle architecture → unit economics + value-perception math (Hormozi side) + premium discipline (Rubin side)  
\- Content brief → single truth, single visual, single CTA  
\- Product page copy / brand copy → tight, declarative, restrained  
\- Singapore expansion sub-questions → reference existing runbook, build forward  
\- Sub-brand decisions → tested against whether they protect or dilute the parent  
\- Customer experience / packaging / unboxing → brand-amplification value \> cost  
  
\#\# Strategic posture  
  
The asset is the brand, not the product. The brand is the minimalism. Most growth requests will be additions; the right answer is often subtraction. Protect the quiet.
