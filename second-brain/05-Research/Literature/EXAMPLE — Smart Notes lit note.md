# EXAMPLE — Smart Notes lit note

**Source:** [[EXAMPLE — How to Take Smart Notes]] · **Status:** done

## Summary
Writing is thinking. A slip-box of atomic, linked notes externalizes your
thinking so ideas can collide and compound over time, instead of being re-derived.

## Key points
- One note = one idea, in your own words.
- Links over folders; let notes find each other.
- The slip-box is a thinking partner, not an archive.

## Quotes
> "The slip-box is not a collection of notes. Working with it is less about
> retrieving specific notes and more about being pointed to relevant facts."

## My take / questions
- The hard part is the *daily* habit of promoting captures into permanent notes.
