# Japandi bedroom spec (lit)

**Source:** [[Japandi Bedroom Design Spec]] · **Status:** done

## Summary
Warm-minimalist redesign worked to an existing room (warm timber floor, white
walls, greenery window, built-in console). The existing materials already supply
~70% of a Japandi palette; the plan leans into them rather than fighting them.

## Key points
- **Palette ratio:** ~70% warm white + wood · ~20% clay/oat accent · ~10% sage + sumi-ink charcoal. Colour comes from materials and light, not pigment.
  - Warm soft white walls `#F1ECE1`; clay/oat accent `#D6CBB8`; charcoal `#2E2B28`; walnut wood `#B07A52`; muted sage `#AFB59B`.
- **Lighting:** three warm 2700K layers — ambient (dimmable downlights), task (nightstand lamps), mood (floor lamp/lantern), plus diffused daylight via sheer linen.
- **Layout:** bed on long wall clear of door swing; convert built-in console to a window reading nook (shakkei — borrowed scenery); keep centre floor open ("empty floor is the design").
- **Budget:** phased, ~IDR 21.6–39.9 jt (~$1,370–2,485) across Foundation → Comfort → Storage/decor.
- **Three protective rules:** match new wood to existing wood; biggest cheap wins = 2700K bulbs+dimmer and clay accent wall; keep the floor open.

## My take / questions
- The "biggest impact for least money" lever (warm bulbs + dimmer) is a generally transferable design principle.
