# decide (skill)

> Full source of the **decide** skill. Catalog: [[_Skills-MOC]].

**description:** Run a structured decision framework for big yes/no or fork-in-the-road
calls ("should I do this", "help me decide", "is this worth it", "what would you do").
Routes through Naval (leverage), Hormozi (math), Rubin (taste) → synthesized verdict.

## The three lenses
1. **Naval — leverage & reversibility:** what leverage does it build/consume (labour/capital/code/media)? one-way vs two-way door? 5-yr horizon; opportunity cost; status vs wealth.
2. **Hormozi — math & unit economics:** cost (money+time+opportunity); realistic upside; payback; cash-flow impact; downside floor; offer-side (margin) vs distribution-side (volume).
3. **Rubin — taste & truth:** does it feel right? addition-for-its-own-sake vs genuine service? quieter/simpler version? protects or dilutes the asset?

## Output shape
1. **Verdict line** (one sentence: Yes / No / Yes-but-only-if / you're-asking-the-wrong-question).
2. Three lens summaries (one paragraph each, in that advisor's voice).
3. Honest tradeoff (give up if yes / give up if no).
4. The principle (Naval-style rule it instantiates).
5. The next move (one concrete action this week).

**Tone:** direct, no hedging; surface advisor disagreement; **default bias toward subtraction** (most calls should be "no" / "not yet").

## Entity-leverage stack (pulled when a decision touches an entity)
- **ANI** — bounded by geology + regulation; capital-and-labour leverage.
- **MMI** — capital + judgment leverage; possibly his **highest-leverage** entity.
- **IJBA** — pure labour leverage; *"question whether it's a business or an expensive hobby."*
- **Glu** — code + media leverage; **highest theoretical ceiling**.
- **Portfolio** — pure capital leverage; should grow in weight over time.
> "Aero cannot grow all five. Most decisions are implicitly about which to underweight."
