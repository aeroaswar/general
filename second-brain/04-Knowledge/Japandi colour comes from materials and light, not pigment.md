# Japandi colour comes from materials and light, not pigment

In a Japandi / warm-minimalist room the colour budget is roughly 70% warm white +
wood, 20% clay/oat, 10% sage + ink. The richness reads from natural materials
(timber tones, linen, stoneware) and warm 2700K light — not from bright painted
surfaces. The cheapest, highest-impact moves are therefore tonal: warm dimmable
bulbs and a single muted accent wall shift the whole mood before any furniture
arrives.

## Why it matters
Reframes a redesign as material + light selection rather than colour selection.
It also predicts the fastest way to *break* the look: mismatched wood tones — so
matching new wood to existing wood matters more than adding any new colour.
