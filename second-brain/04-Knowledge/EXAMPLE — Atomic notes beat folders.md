# Atomic notes beat folders

A note should hold **one idea**, stated in your own words, and stand on its own.
Retrieval then comes from *links*, not from remembering which folder you filed it in.
Folders force a single location; links let one idea live in many contexts at once.

## Why it matters
Folders scale badly — every note has exactly one home, so cross-topic ideas get
lost. A densely linked graph surfaces connections you forgot you made, which is
where new thinking actually comes from.
